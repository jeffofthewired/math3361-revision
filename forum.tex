    \item \textcolor{question}{
        Ito Isometry Question
    }

    The statement that
    $$\lim_{n\to\infty}\mathbb E[|I_{f_n}(t)|^2] = \mathbb E[|I_f(t)|^2]$$
    does not come from the reasoning in the proof.
    
    Instead it comes from the general fact that if we have a 
    sequence $(X_n)_{n\in\mathbb N}$ of random variables that converges in $L^2$ to
    the random variable $X$, then
    $$\lim_{n\to\infty} \lVert X_n\rVert_{L^2} = \lVert X\rVert_{L^2}.$$

    Here, our sequence of random variables is $(I_{f_n}(t))_{n\in\mathbb N}$,
    and they converge in $L^2$ to $I_{f}(t)$ \\(this is by the definition of the It\^o integral).
    

\end{enumerate}
